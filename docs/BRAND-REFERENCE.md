# Curly Hair Collective — Brand Reference Guide

## Brand Identity

### Name
**Curly Hair Collective**

### Tagline
*"Every curl has a story."*

### Mission Statement
Celebrating curly hair culture through conversations, community, and confidence. We are the platform where every curl, coil, and wave is celebrated — through expert-led podcast episodes, educational content, and a vibrant community.

### Vision
To be the #1 destination for curly hair culture — a space where textured hair is not just accepted but celebrated, studied, and championed.

| Trait | Description |

## Voice & Tone

### Brand Voice (Consistent)

- **Warm & Welcoming**: Like talking to your best friend who happens to be a curl expert
- **Knowledgeable but Accessible**: We break down the science without being condescending
- **Bold & Confident**: We don't apologize for taking up space
- **Inclusive**: Every curl pattern, every texture, every background

### Tone Variations by Context

| Context | Tone |
|---------|------|
| Podcast episodes | Conversational, curious, empathetic |
| Blog/articles | Educational, clear, practical |
| Social media | Playful, trendy, community-oriented |
| Merch copy | Bold, fun, empowering |
| Sponsor communications | Professional, data-driven, partnership-focused |
| Email newsletters | Friendly, personal, value-packed |

### Writing Guidelines

- Use "we" and "our" — we're in this together
- Address the reader as "you" — make it personal
- Embrace colloquialisms: "wash day," "curl pattern," "good hair day," "crown"
- Use power phrases: "Your curls are your crown," "Wear it proudly," "Curl queen"
- Avoid: "good hair" vs "bad hair," anything that ranks curl patterns, product shaming
- Keep sentences punchy. Mix short and long for rhythm.
- Use questions to engage: "Ever had a bad wash day?" "What's your curl type?"

## Logo Usage

### Primary Logo
- CurlIcon (stylized curl strand) + "Curly Hair Collective" wordmark
- Minimum clear space: 1x the height of the curl icon on all sides

### Logo Variations
- **Full Logo**: Icon + wordmark (horizontal)
- **Icon Only**: CurlIcon for small applications (favicons, avatars)
- **Wordmark Only**: Text-only for inline references

### Color Applications
- **On light backgrounds**: Pink 500 icon, gradient-pink text
- **On dark backgrounds**: Pink 300 icon, Pink 200 text
- **On gradient backgrounds**: White icon, white text

### Don't
- Stretch or distort the logo
- Change the curl direction
- Use unapproved colors
- Place on busy backgrounds without sufficient contrast

## Photography & Imagery Direction

### Style
- Natural, unretouched hair photography
- Warm, golden-hour lighting preferred
- Diverse representation across all curl patterns (2A-4C)
- Lifestyle-oriented: real moments, not overly posed
- Soft pink/gold color grading where appropriate

### Subjects
- Women of all backgrounds with natural textured hair
- Close-ups of curl patterns and textures
- Wash day and styling process shots
- Community and togetherness moments
- Product flat-lays with on-brand styling

### Don't
- Over-edit or smooth hair texture
- Use stock photos that feel inauthentic
- Show only one curl type or ethnicity
- Use harsh, clinical lighting

## Content Pillars

### 1. Curl Care & Education
- Wash day routines, product reviews, science-backed tips
- Protein vs moisture, ingredient education, styling techniques
- Seasonal adjustments, protective styling, nighttime care

### 2. Culture & Identity
- CROWN Act and hair discrimination
- Hair in the workplace and schools
- Historical and cultural significance of textured hair
- Personal stories and identity journeys

### 3. Community & Empowerment
- Guest spotlights, listener stories
- Community events and meetups
- Confidence building, self-love journeys
- Curly kids and family content

### 4. Lifestyle & Beauty
- Fashion and style for curly-haired individuals
- Wellness and self-care routines
- Beauty beyond hair — skincare, makeup for melanin-rich skin
- Travel tips for textured hair
